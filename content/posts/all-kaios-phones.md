## Notable KaiOS Phones

Of the 100+ KaiOS phones sold around the world, a few are superlative and worth noting. While Reliance's [JioPhone]({{< ref "whats-next-jiophone#jiophone" >}} "JioPhone") is undoubtedly the global best seller with well over 100M units sold, several other models made history.

### The Prequel: The GreatCall Jitterbug Flip

![GreatCall Jitterbug Flip](/img/jitterbug-flip.jpg "GreatCall Jitterbug Flip")

The GreatCall Jitterbug Flip (aka Alcatel Go Flip) was a very simple flip phone designed for senior citizens. It's big buttons, large text, and simple menu made navigation a breeze. When it was released it 2016, it ran <u>[Firefox OS 2.2](https://www.engadget.com/2019-02-26-kaios-third-mobile-operating-system.html)</u>, the predecessor to KaiOS. The Jitterbug Flip was later updated to KaiOS 2.5, in part, to deliver VoLTE support. Despite not being marketed with KaiOS, in a way, **the Jitterbug Flip was the "first" KaiOS phone**.

### The First: Alcatel Go Flip 2

![Alcatel Go Flip 2](/img/alcatel-go-flip-4044.jpg "Alcatel Go Flip 2")

The **first official KaiOS phone was the Alcatel Go Flip 2**. Released in 2019, it supported all major North American carriers (e.g., Sprint, T-Mobile, AT&T), this launch proved KaiOS is stable, secure, and scalable. Otherwise, from the outside the Alcatel Go Flip 2 is nearly identical to all other Go Flip models. Other variants including the Cingular Flip 2 and Alcatel OneTouch (4044) ran **KaiOS 1.0 based on [Gecko 37](https://sites.google.com/view/bananahackers/devices/alcatel-ot-4044) and lacked the KaiStore**.

### The First with KaiOS 3.0: Alcatel Go Flip 4 and TCL Flip Pro

![Alcatel Go Flip 4 and TCL Flip Pro](/img/alcatel-go-flip-tcl-flip-pro.jpeg "Alcatel Go Flip 4 and TCL Flip Pro")

Released in [2021](https://www.phonearena.com/news/tcl-flip-pro-and-alcatel-go-flip4-release-price_id135116) at the same time, **the TCL Flip Pro (Verizon) and Alcatel Go Flip 4 (T-Mobile) were the first with KaiOS 3.0**. Each sported a Qualcomm 215 and were noticeably snappier than KaiOS 2.5 devices. KaiOS 3.0 was based on Gecko 84 and isn't backwards compatible with KaiOS 2.5. As a result, each device launched without support for major applications like Facebook or WhatsApp (which are still not supported in 2024).

### KaiOS 3.0 Outside USA: Orbic Journey Pro 4G

![Orbic Journey Pro 4G](/img/orbic-journey-pro-4g.jpg "Orbic Journey Pro 4G")

Three years after it's initial release, KaiOS 3.1 finally made an appearance outside of the United States with the launch of the [Orbic Journey Pro 4G in Japan](https://k-tai.watch.impress.co.jp/docs/event/mwc2024/1572811.html). What's more, it's **KaiOS' first phone sold in Japan!**

### KaiOS Home Phone: Kammunica La Mano

![Kammunica La Mano](/img/kammunica-la-mano.jpeg "Kammunica La Mano")

Kammunica's La Mano is the first "fixed wireless phone" using KaiOS. It's essentially a landline home phone that makes calls wirelessly over Wi-Fi (VoWiFi) and 4G LTE (VoLTE) and comes with a charging dock. Otherwise it runs KaiOS 2.5 indistinguishable from other phones.

### The First with KaiOS 4.0: TCL Flip 4

![TCL Flip 4 5G](/img/tcl-flip-4.jpg "TCL Flip 4 5G")

Released in [May 2025](https://www.howtogeek.com/tcl-flip-4-5g-t-mobile-kaios/), **the TCL Flip 4  is the first with KaiOS 4.0 and 5G**. Launched on [Metro by T-Mobile](https://www.metrobyt-mobile.com/cell-phone/tcl-flip-4-5g), it sports a Qualcomm Snapdragon 4S SoC and a noticeably larger 3000mAh battery (likely because 5G consumes substantially more power than 4G). The TCL Flip 4 largely met [predictions for KaiOS 4.0]({{< ref "./kaios-4.0-upcoming" >}}) including a Qualcomm 5G ship, an upgraded Gecko engine and Android base OS, and improved eSIM support.
